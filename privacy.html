Mindlayer

Privacy Policy

Mindlayer runs entirely on your device. It has no internet access and sends no data to the developer or any third party.

Effective date: 6 June 2026  ·  Last updated: 15 July 2026

The short version. Mindlayer is a fully on-device, offline AI service. It declares no internet permission, collects no personal data, contains no analytics, advertising, or tracking, and transmits nothing to the developer or any third party. Its AI processing stays on your device; apps you approve receive the results they request and have their own permissions and privacy practices.

1. Who this policy covers

This Privacy Policy applies to the Mindlayer Android application (package com.adsamcik.mindlayer), published by adsamcik ("we", "us"). Mindlayer is a background service that loads an on-device large language model and lets other apps on the same device run AI inference (text generation, text embeddings, and optical character recognition) locally, without a network connection. It also includes a diagnostics dashboard for you to inspect the service's status.

2. No internet access — by design

Mindlayer does not request the INTERNET permission. The packaged app includes ACCESS_NETWORK_STATE, declared by bundled Android scheduling components, which permits reading connectivity state but does not permit sending or receiving network data. Without INTERNET, Android prevents the app from opening network connections. There is no cloud component, no backend server, and no remote API.

3. Information we collect

We collect no personal information. Because the app cannot access the network, no data of any kind — personal or otherwise — is sent off your device by Mindlayer. Specifically, Mindlayer contains:

  • no analytics or usage-statistics SDKs;
  • no crash-reporting services;
  • no advertising or advertising identifiers;
  • no third-party tracking or social SDKs;
  • no account system, sign-in, or user profile.

4. How your data is processed (on your device only)

When an app on your device asks Mindlayer to run AI inference, the inputs and outputs are processed on-device and are never transmitted off the device by Mindlayer. Results are returned to the requesting app. The table below describes how each kind of data is handled.

DataWhere it livesHow long it is kept
Prompts and other text you (or an app) provide Request buffers and session context in device memory (RAM) Request-scoped buffers are released when the request ends. Successful prompts and output may remain as session context until the session ends, expires, is evicted, the client disconnects, or the process stops.
Images and audio sent for analysis Staged encrypted at rest (AES-256-GCM with a key that exists only in memory) in the app's private cache, because the on-device model reads media by file path Deleted when the request ends; any leftover ciphertext becomes permanently undecryptable once the process exits and the cache is cleared on the next start
Synchronous model output (generated text, embeddings, recognized text) Returned to the requesting app in memory Not persisted to service storage. It may remain in service memory as session context until the session ends, expires, is evicted, the client disconnects, or the process stops.
Deferred model output A local, encrypted database (SQLCipher) on your device; large embedding results may use encrypted private files Fetching marks a result delivered but does not by itself erase its encrypted record. Acknowledgement deletes it. Deferred results are not guaranteed to survive disconnection of the requesting app; large embedding-result files may be removed when that client disconnects. Records may also be quota-evicted or expire after 24 hours by default; expired records are removed during a later deferred operation.
Diagnostic logs A local, encrypted database (SQLCipher) on your device Automatically deleted after seven days during startup and daily cleanup, or earlier when you clear app storage or uninstall. Logs contain only metadata — timing, token counts, request and session identifiers, error labels, and for security decisions, caller identity metadata such as UIDs, package identifiers, and signing-certificate hash prefixes. They never contain your prompt text, images, audio, or model output.
Consent and authorization metadata App-private files protected against tampering with HMAC signatures and coordinated with file locks. Records can include package and display names, signing-certificate hashes, install source, decisions, timestamps, and consent-attempt counters. Approvals remain until revoked. Pending challenges expire and are pruned during later challenge operations. Temporary denials stop affecting authorization at expiry but may remain in the signed file until a later denial write. Choosing “Not now” increments the consent-attempt counter; counters can remain after cooldown and are cleared by a grant, 24-hour denial, or permanent block. Permanent blocks remain until removed. Clearing app storage or uninstalling removes all records.

A requesting app may choose to keep its own conversation history in its own encrypted storage on your device (for crash-recovery and continuity). That history belongs to, and is controlled by, that app — not by Mindlayer — and still never leaves the device through Mindlayer.

5. Data sharing

We do not share any data off-device, because no data is collected or transmitted over a network. Mindlayer has no servers and no third-party integrations that receive data. We do not sell, rent, or disclose personal information to anyone.

6. Permissions we request and why

PermissionWhy it is needed
FOREGROUND_SERVICE / FOREGROUND_SERVICE_SPECIAL_USE To keep the AI model running while it actively serves a visible request, so inference is not killed mid-response.
POST_NOTIFICATIONS To show the required foreground-service notification while inference is running.
USE_BIOMETRIC To request Android device authentication before granting or revoking access when preflight succeeds. If preflight reports no enrollment, no hardware, or unavailable hardware, the explicit Mindlayer confirmation screen is used without an OS auth prompt. Other preflight failures deny the action.
USE_FINGERPRINT For backward-compatible device authentication support on older Android versions.
HIDE_OVERLAY_WINDOWS To protect the approval screen from tap-jacking overlays so you always see exactly which app you are granting access to.
ACCESS_NETWORK_STATE Declared by bundled Android scheduling components for connectivity constraints. It can read connectivity state but cannot transmit data; Mindlayer does not request INTERNET.
WAKE_LOCK / RECEIVE_BOOT_COMPLETED Declared by bundled Android scheduling components so scheduled local work can complete reliably and be rescheduled after a device restart.
FOREGROUND_SERVICE_DATA_SYNC Declared by bundled on-device model-delivery and NPU components for foreground model extraction or processing. It does not grant network access.

None of these permissions grant access to your contacts, location, messages, files, or any other personal data, and none enable network access.

7. Your control: consent, retention, and deletion

  • Consent. No app can use Mindlayer until you explicitly approve it on a confirmation screen that shows the requesting app's name, signing certificate, and install source. Device authentication is requested when Android preflight succeeds. No enrollment, no hardware, or unavailable hardware uses explicit confirmation without an OS prompt; other preflight failures deny the action. You can deny or block any app.
  • In-memory data. Request-scoped buffers are released when a request ends. Successful prompts and output may remain in session context until that session ends, expires, is evicted, the client disconnects, or the process stops.
  • Local logs and any cached files can be removed at any time from Android Settings → Apps → Mindlayer → Storage → Clear storage.
  • Full removal. Uninstalling Mindlayer deletes all of its on-device data, including the encrypted log database and any staged files.

8. Security

Mindlayer is designed to protect your information on the device:

  • media staged for the model is encrypted at rest with AES-256-GCM using a key that never leaves memory;
  • diagnostic logs are stored in an encrypted (SQLCipher) database;
  • each app must be individually approved by you; Android device authentication is requested when preflight succeeds; no enrollment, no hardware, or unavailable hardware uses explicit confirmation without an OS prompt, while other preflight failures deny the action;
  • the approval screen blocks screenshots and screen recording to prevent leaking sensitive identity and signing-certificate details;
  • on-device model files are integrity-checked (SHA-256) before use.

No method of storage is 100% secure, but because Mindlayer makes no network transmissions, your data is never exposed in network transit or on a server.

9. Children's privacy

Mindlayer does not target children and does not knowingly collect any personal information from anyone, including children under 13 (or the equivalent minimum age in your jurisdiction). Because the app collects and transmits no data, it poses no data-collection risk to users of any age.

10. Third-party services

Mindlayer uses only on-device, open-source AI runtimes and models (such as Google's LiteRT and Gemma, and PaddleOCR) that run locally and make no network calls. It integrates no third-party services that receive your data.
